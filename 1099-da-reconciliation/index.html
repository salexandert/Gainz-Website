1099-DA preparation

Prepare for broker-reported crypto data without losing the source story.

Gainz is useful when broker forms, exchange CSVs, holdings, and prior-year evidence do not line up cleanly. It helps organize the work needed before relying on a filing number.

Important

Gainz does not provide tax advice or direct filing. Treat 1099-DA review as evidence organization for professional review.

What to reconcile

Broker forms are only one part of the record.

Proceeds

Compare broker-reported proceeds against imported sale and disposal records.

Cost basis

Identify missing acquisition history instead of assuming broker-reported proceeds include complete basis.

Transfers

Review sends and receives that may explain why one platform lacks the full basis story.

Evidence

Use reference-only evidence records and a CPA packet to keep supporting files organized.

When Gainz helps

Best fit: messy historical records.

If your forms and CSVs already reconcile cleanly, a hosted tax product may be faster. If you need to explain gaps, missing basis, old transfers, or prior-year evidence, Gainz is built for that review path.

Auditable handoff

Trace a missing-basis decision to its source and impact.

Broker proceeds

Preserve gross proceeds, fees, and net proceeds from the imported source row.

Documented lots

Apply supported FIFO acquisition basis before isolating any unresolved quantity.

Professional workpaper

Record the exact treatment, evidence reference, named direction, assumptions, and before/after Form 8949 impact.

Material assumptions

Surface calculation-changing treatments in packet preview, packet status, CPA handoff notes, and workbook status.
